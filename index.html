The SurgQ Study

Led by the IndSurg Collaborative and WHO Collaborating Centre for Emergency, Critical and Operative Care

Study Title

Comprehensive Assessment of Surgical Care Quality (SurgQ) for Abdominal Surgeries performed at Secondary and Tertiary Care Centers in India

Study Design

A prospective, cross-sectional, multicentre observational study measuring quality indicators in real-time

Population

Patients aged 18 years and older undergoing major abdominal surgeries at secondary and tertiary care centers

Initiative Type

An investigator-initiated, investigator-led, and unfunded collaborative research study

In India, a substantial gap exists in our understanding of surgical care quality due to limited resources, lack of standardized protocols, and fragmented care pathways. The SurgQ study adapts a proven 15-indicator quality assessment tool to the Indian context, measuring safety, effectiveness, timeliness, patient-centeredness, equity, and efficiency.

Our Mission

Primary Aim

To systematically assess the quality of surgical care being provided across secondary and tertiary Indian hospitals by measuring evidence-based indicators

Key Objectives

  • To estimate baseline surgical quality indicators across India
  • To test the feasibility of data collection using an LMIC-specific assessment tool
  • To assess variation in care quality across public/private and urban/rural hospitals

Participant Eligibility

Inclusion Criteria

  • Patients aged 18 years and above
  • Undergoing elective abdominal surgeries
  • Undergoing emergency abdominal surgeries
  • Including obstructed/strangulated inguinal hernia cases

Exclusion Criteria

  • Patients operated outside the study site
  • Patients referred for post-operative management
  • Elective inguinal hernia repair cases
